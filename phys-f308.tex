In this course, we are considering a large class of materials featuring certain properties. In particular, we are interested in materials that are "soft" such as simple liquids, polymers, gels, glasses, liquid crystals, ... or even biological materials!
\begin{definition}
    A particular object is defined to be soft matter when its interactions are of the order of $k_BT$. 
\end{definition}
Being defined at such an order, \emph{soft matter} presents a series of particularly interesting properties:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Lack of long-range order.
    \item Relevance of the entropic contribution to the energy balance.
    \item Corrections from quantum mechanics not needed.
\end{itemize}
